# Importing Episodes

Loading a video into AMVerge splits it into individual scenes automatically and
shows them in the clip grid.

## Import a video

1. Click **Import Episode** in the top toolbar (or drag a file onto the window).
2. Pick a video file.
3. Wait while AMVerge reads the video and builds the clip grid. The button shows
   **Processing...** during import.

## Supported formats

Common video files work, including `mp4`, `mkv`, `webm`, and more. Anime
episodes, movies, or any video can be imported.

## How AMVerge splits episodes

AMVerge splits videos at **I-Frames (keyframes)**. It does *not* use traditional
frame-by-frame scene detection.

- **What are I-Frames?** Full image frames placed throughout a video, usually
  around cuts or major visual changes.
- **Why use them?** Splitting at keyframes is near-instant and needs no full
  re-encode, so importing stays fast and clips are a 1:1 copy (no quality loss).
- **Downside:** Some encodes place I-Frames in odd spots, creating short or
  duplicate clips. Those can be [merged back together](/docs/merging-scenes).

## Import vs. storage

Importing copies processed clip data into AMVerge's storage so you can reopen
past imports quickly. It never touches or deletes your original video files.

You can change where this data lives, see Episodes Storage Path in
[General Settings](/docs/settings-general).
