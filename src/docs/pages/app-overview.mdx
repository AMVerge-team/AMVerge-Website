# Interface Overview

AMVerge is a desktop app for editors who need to split a video into usable clips, browse them visually, and export selections fast, without a traditional timeline.

This page maps out the workspace so the rest of the guide makes sense.

<Media src="/clips/Sousou no Frieren - 01_0125.gif" caption="Each tile in the grid is one detected scene." />

## The workspace

| Region | Location | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Sidebar** | Left | Home / Menu / Settings nav, plus the Episode Panel |
| **Clip grid** | Center | Every scene of the active episode, as a grid of clips |
| **Preview panel** | Right | Focused clip preview + export controls |
| **Top bar** | Top | App title, Discord link, HEVC status, grid column control |

## Sidebar

The sidebar holds the three top-level pages and the Episode Panel:

- **Home** — your main workspace: the clip grid and preview panel.
- **Menu** — About, Console (errors/logs), Patch Notes, and Credits.
- **Settings** — general options and appearance customization.

Below the nav is the [Episode Panel](/docs/app-episode-panel), where imported episodes and folders live.

## Clip grid

The center grid shows one clip per detected scene. From here you browse,
hover to preview, and [select clips](/docs/selecting-clips). The number of
columns is set from the **Grid: N columns** control in the top right.

## Preview panel

The right panel shows the currently focused clip and all the
[export controls](/docs/exporting): export profile, destination, merge toggle,
and the **Export Now** button. A collapsible **How To Use** card sits at the
bottom.

## Recommended workflow

1. **Import** an episode or movie.
2. **Browse** the grid and grab anything useful, don't overthink it early.
3. **Merge** awkwardly split scenes back together.
4. **Export** your selection.
